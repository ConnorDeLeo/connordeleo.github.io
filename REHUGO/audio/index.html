Podcast Episode 1:

Rhetorical Precis:

James Pumphrey and Nolan Sykes, in the podcast Past Gas, Episode 5 “Unraveling the Mystery of Japan's SECRET Racing Club”, explains that the Midnight Club was extremely important to Japanese car culture. Pumphrey and Sykes support their claim by illustrating how they influenced street racing and car culture as a whole. The speakers’ purpose is to show how the Midnight Club was important in order to preserve the history of the club. The author uses a casual, yet informative, tone for their large audience of car enthusiasts.

Podcast Episode 2:

Rhetorical Precis:

James Pumphrey and Nolan Sykes, in the podcast Past Gas, Episode 6 “What Ended the Golden Age of Japanese Street Racing?”, explains that the fall of Japanese street racing was important for safety, but sad for automotive enthusiasts. Pumphrey and Sykes support their claim by describing how the golden age of street racing in Japan influenced car culture and how it fell. The speakers’ purpose is to show how street racing was important in order to show off the rich history of Japanese automobiles. The author uses a casual, yet informative, tone for their large audience of car enthusiasts.

Review:

Both of these Past Gas podcast episodes are related to Japanese street racing and the Midnight Club. In the first episode, James Pumphrey and Nolan Sykes demystify the Midnight Club by showing off its full history, from how it was started all the way to the downfall of Midnight Club. James and Nolan also go through the dangers of street racing, but also how exhilarating it could be. They also cover how it relates to Japanese car culture. In the second episode, Pumphrey and Sykes cover the general fall of street racing in Japan as the year closed onto 2000 and the unfortunate accident that caused the downfall of the Midnight Club. In the second episode James and Nolan also went over the Midnight Club and street racing’s general affect over Japanese car culture and its lead into 2000s The Fast and The Furious series, a movie series greatly influenced by Japanese street racing culture with the massive amount of Japanese tuner vehicles such as those in the initial scene of the first movie containing a Mazda FD Rx-7, a Honda Civic Type-R, a Acura (or Honda in Japan) Integra, and a Mitsubishi Eclipse. The Fast and The Furious were heavily influenced by the 1980s and 1990s Japanese street racing as was much of the early 2000s era car culture. Another media greatly influenced by the Midnight Club that James and Nolan speak about was Wangan Midnight, an anime and manga where hashiriya (Japanese word for street racer) raced on the Wangan highway in tuned cars. Both of the Past Gas episodes released information about how the Midnight Club and Japanese street racing influenced car culture and its history.
