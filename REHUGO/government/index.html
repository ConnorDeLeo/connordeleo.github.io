Vingilis, E., Smart, R. G., Mann, R. E., Paglia-Boak, A., Stoduto, G., & Adlaf, E. M. (n.d.). Prevalence and Correlates of Street Racing Among Ontario High School Students. Taylor & Francis Online. https://www-tandfonline-com.csulb.idm.oclc.org/doi/abs/10.1080/15389588.2011.602148

Vingilis, Smart, Mann, Paglia-Boak, Stoduto, and Adlaf cover the relationship between street racing and Ontario high school students. They used anonymous self reports of street racing and multiple other factors including grade level, rural/urban, school marks, cannabis use, drinking and driving, cannabis use and driving, and property, physical, drugs, and weapons delinquencies. These data points were used in order to determine the correlation between street racing and legal backgrounds. The results concluded that males in grades 11 and 12 that were participating in other illegal activities were far more likely to street race than all other groups of people.
